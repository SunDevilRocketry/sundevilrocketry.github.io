A Rocket Project - Straw Rockets

SUMMARY

Rockets are complex machines which are being developed to continue pushing the bounds of humanity. While it is difficult to make a rocket at home, the straw rocket experiment offers the experience of rocket building and only takes about 5 minutes to build.

Scroll down to view the step to vuild your very own straw rocket...

Materials:

The following project requires a couple of household items to do:

  • Paper (for rocket)
  • Pencil
  • Straw (for launcher)
  • Scissors/Tape
  • Markers, Color Pencils, Crayons: OPTIONAL

Instructions

Step 1: Print

Click on the link button below and print out the following NASA document.

Straw Rocket PDF

Step 2: Cut out and shape the rocket body + fins

Cut out the rectangle. This will be the body tube of the rocket. Wrap the rectangle around a wooden pencil length-wise and tape the rectangle so that it forms a tube and holds its shape.

Step 3: Cut out and attach the fins

Cut out the two fin units. Align the bottom of the rectangle that extends between the fins with the end of the rocket body, and tape the fin to the body tube. Do the same thing for the other fin on the opposite side, making a “fin sandwich.”

Step 4: Make the rocket nose cone

Twist the top of the body tube into a nose cone around the sharpened end of your pencil and tape the top opening of the body tube shut. During this process, you should minimize any gaps created by the making of the noze cone.

Straw Rocket PDF

Step 5: Prepare to launch!

Remove the pencil and replace it with a soda straw. Be sure your launch area is sclear of people and hazards. Then, blow into the straw to launch your rocket!
